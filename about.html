beans to bars process

❮ ❯

  1. Step 1: Sorting

    Hand sorting raw cocoa beans to remove poor quality beans

  2. Step 2: Roasting

    Slow roasting the cocoa beans at low temps to enhance natural flavors

  3. Step 3: Cracking

    Cracking the roasted beans to get to the cacao nibs ("meat" of the bean) inside; Winnowing away the lighter-weight husk using a vacuum system

  4. Step 4: Grinding

    Slowly grinding the nibs with stone wheels until the pressure and heat turn the nibs into a liquid (100% dark chocolate for those counting)

  5. Step 5: Flavoring

    Adding sugar and a little cocoa butter (which we press in-house), then continuing to grind until we have smooth, creamy chocolate

  6. Step 6: Conching

    Conching the liquid chocolate to reduce natural acidity and enhance flavors and texture

  7. Step 7: Tempering

    Tempering the chocolate (scientific stuff...to properly align the crystals) to lock in a great shine, snap and texture

  8. Step 8: Molding

    Pour into molds to create truffles and chocolate bars which are then hand wrapped
